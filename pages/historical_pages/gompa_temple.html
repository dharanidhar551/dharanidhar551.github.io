Gompa Temple Gompa Buddhist Temple, located in Itanagar, is one of the major tourist attractions of Itanagar as well as Arunachal Pradesh. Often called the Buddhist studies, this place is situated on top of a small hill offering the best scenic views of the city. The Gompa Buddhist Temple may be of varied interests. One can go there to worship, connect with Buddha and his teachings. One can go to absorb the highly colourful and welcoming nature. It can easily be a good spot to meditate. A Gompa also known as ling is a Buddhist religious defences of learning, lineage and sadhana that may be understood as a conflation of a defence. The specialty of Gompa Temple has two main chambers. The monastery is painted in red, ochre and white and houses 60 Iamas. It has an attached nunnery. It was built as a fort monastery in the Central Tibetan Pattern. Gompa Temple is located close to the state Museum Road and can be easily be easily be accessed by public transport. Prominent attractions of the city like State Museum and Ita Fort. By Air: Lilabari airport in North Lakhimpur is the nearest airport to the Itanagar at a distance of about 57 kilometers. Lilabari airport receives regional Air India flights from Kolkata and Guwahati. By Train: Harmuti railway station in Assam and Naharlagun railway station in Arunachal Pradesh are the closest railway to reach Itanagar, each at a distance of 34 kilometers and 15 kilometers respectively. By Road: You can reach Itanagar via bus from Banderdewa, North Lakhimpur, Tezpur and Guwahati.
