About

Amaravathi, the new capital of Andhra Pradesh, also known as “City of Tolerance” became world famous because of the Amareshwara Temple and also the site for one of the greatest Buddhist Stupas ever built. Amaravathi, popularly known as People’s capital has many beautiful views and is a great place for devotion for Hindus and Buddhists. Being part of several different dynasties over centuries, Amaravathi has a miscellaneous culture which is rich and diverse. At a distance of 33 km from Guntur, 38 km from Vijayawada, 111 km from Machilipatnam, 162 km from Khammam, 178 km from Nalgonda, 178 km from Bhadrachalam, 194 km from Rajahmundry, 270 km from Nellore, 274 km from Hyderabad, 403 km from Vishakhapatnam, 450 km from Anantapur, Amaravathi is a popular historical town situated on the southern bank of Krishna River in Guntur district of Andhra Pradesh. Some of the popular places to visit nearby are Amaravathi Archaeological Museum, Mangalagiri Temple, Amaravathi Mahachaitya, Undavalli Caves, and Kondaveedu Fort. There are a few accommodation options available in Amaravathi whereas Vijayawada and Guntur are the nearest places to get the better accommodation options that range from budget to luxury hotels. Vijayawada International Airport is the nearest airport which is about 59 km from Amaravathi. It has well flight connectivity with all major cities. Vijayawada Junction, about 38 km, is the nearest railhead to Amaravathi which has well rail connectivity with all the cities. Amaravathi experiences a tropical climate, with hot summers and mild winters. The best time for a visit to Amaravathi is in the winters, especially the months from October to March when the climate is pleasant and the ambiance is lovely. The summers are unbearably hot, and the monsoons, although lovely and inviting, might dampen your plans if the rainfall is more than normal.
