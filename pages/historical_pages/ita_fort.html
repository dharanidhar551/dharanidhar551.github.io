Ita Fort Ita Fort, located in Itanagar town, is one of the most important historical sites in the state of Arunachal Pradesh, India. The name literally means “Fort of bricks” (brick being called Ita).It was built as early as 14th or 15th century by kings of Chutia dynasty. The fort has an irregular shape, built mainly with bricks dating back to the 14th-15th century. The total brickwork is of 16,200 cubic meter length which was probably built by kings of Chutiya kingdom which ruled the region during that time. The fort has three different entrances at three different sides, which are western, the eastern and the southern sides. Nearest places to the Ita Fort are the Ganga Lake and Rupa. Ganga Lake is also known as the Gyankar Sinyi, this is a major attraction for locals and tourists alike. This is 4 km from city center. Rupa is located 4 km from city center. This is a scenic hill station one must visit during his/her trip to Itanagar. Rupa lies on the banks of Tenga and provides tourists with the most brilliant sight ever. By Air: Itanagar doesn’t have an airport of its own. The nearest airport located in Itanagar is the Lilabari airport located in north Lakhimpur in Assam. This airport is located about 67 km from the capital city. By Rail: Itanagar does not have a railway station of its own. The closest rail head is located in Harmuty in Assam. The railway station is about 33 km away from Itanagar. By Road: Itanagar has an excellent network of roads. Itanagar is connected to all the major cities and other adjacent states of Manipur, Assam, Tripura Meghalaya and Mizoram.
