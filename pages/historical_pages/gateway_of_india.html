The Gate Way of India The Gate Way of India, an Arch monument, often referred to as “The Taj Mahal of India”, located in Mumbai resembles the finest art of Indian sculpture. As the name suggests it literally served as a gate-way to India for the British Viceroys. It was erected to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder on their visit to India in 1911. The structure is an arch made of basalt, 26 meters (85 feet) high. The structural plan of Gate way of India is designed in the Indo-Saracenic style. The Gateway of India is located on the waterfront at Apollo Bunder area at the end of Chhatrapati Shivaji Marg in South Mumbai and overlooks the Arabian Sea. Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport, formerly known as Sahar International Airport, is the primary international Airport serving the Mumbai Metropolitan Area. It is situated about 30 km from CST Station. Mumbai is very well connected to rest of India by trains. The Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus is the most popular station in Mumbai. Trains to Mumbai are available from all major railway stations in India. Mumbai is well-connected with national highways and expressways. Mumbai visit by bus is the most economical for individual tourists. Some of the nearby tourist attractions are Elephant Caves; Statues of Maratha leader Shivaji and Swami Vivekananda which are installed at the entrance of Elephant caves. The Taj Mahal Hotel which is India’s most prestigious luxurious hotel is situated close to the Gate way of India. Best time to visit is during the period of November to March, as the post-monsoon climate is very pleasant.
