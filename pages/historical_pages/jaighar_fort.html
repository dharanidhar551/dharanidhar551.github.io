Jaigarh Fort Jaigarh Fort is located in Devisinghpura, Amer, Jaipur, Rajasthan. Jaigarh Fort is situated on the promontory called the Cheel ka Teela (Hill of Eagles) of the Aravalli range, it overlooks the Amer Fort and the Maota Lake, near Amer in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. The fort was built by Jai Singh II in 1726 to protect the Amer Fort and its palace complex and was named after him. Jaigarh Fort is a monumental fort located on the Aravalli Ranges of Rajasthan, towering at an impressive height of 500m above sea level. The fort is built with thick walls of red sandstone and is spread over a vast range of 3 kilometers in length, with a width of one kilometer. The fort houses the world's greatest cannon on wheels known as the 'Jaivana Cannon' and a huge palace complex. The nearest places to reach the Jaigarh Fort are Durga Mandir, Jhalana Nature Park, Amer Park and Kanak Vrindavan Mahal. By Air: Jaipur is connected to various major cities by air as the city has its airport at Sanganer which is 12 km away from the city. International and domestic flights land here. The airlines whose national and international flights land in Jaipur are Jet Airways, Air India, Oman Air, SpiceJet, and IndiGo. By Train: Jaipur is connected to various cities in India through trains. Many long and short-distance train start, end, or through Jaipur. Premium trains like Shatabdi, Rajdhani, Duronto, Double Decker, Garib Rath trains connect Jaipur to various cities. By Road: Jaipur is connected to many cities through a network of national highways. Bus services are provided by Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporationto go to and come from Delhi, Agra, Ahmedabad, Ajmer, Udaipur and many other cities.
