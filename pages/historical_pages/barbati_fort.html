Barabati Fort Barabati Fort is located in Biju Patnaik Colony, Cuttack, Odisha in India. Barabati Fort, built by the Ganga dynasty on the banks of River Mahanadi, is one of the most sought-after tourist attractions of Cuttack. According to historians, Barabati Fort once housed a nine-storey palace. While the fort is spread over an area of 102 acres. Historians believe that it was Raja Anangabhimadeva, of the Eastern Gangas who built the fort of Barabati in the beginning of the 13th century. The fort emerged as the imperial centre of the Eastern Gangas who reigned over this politically significant as well as economically prosperous region. The ruins of the fort remain with its moat, gate, and the earthen mound of the nine-storied palace, which evokes the memories of past days. The nearest places to visit the Barabati Fort are Cuttack Chandi Temple, Oriya Baptish Church and Netaji Birth Place. These places are located less than 1 km to the Barabati Fort. By Air: The nearest airport to Cuttack is Biju Patnaik Airport which is located in Bhubaneswar. There are quite a good number of daily flights from different parts of India. The airport is well connected with cities like Hyderabad, Delhi and Chennai. From the airport, one can take private cabs to reach the city. By Train: The city is well connected with all the major parts of the nation through rail networks. Lying on the Kolkata-Chennai line, many super fasts, expresses, and localtrains passing through the city. By Road: Bus services are excellent in Cuttack. The national highway connects the city with the major and nearby cities. One can find buses as well as taxis which will take people in and out the city with ease. Bhubaneshwar is located at a distance of 27 km from this beautiful city.
