Rasmancha Rasmancha is located in Bishnupur, Bankura district, WestBengal, India. It was built by Bir Hamber and established in 1600 AD, the Rasmancha was used to display all the local idols in public during the Ras Festival which stopped in 1932. The popular festival has been shifted to grounds near a Durga temple. The structure of Rasmancha in West Bengal at the upper part looks like a pyramid. Middle part resembles Bengali huts and the arches of the lower part resembles Islamic architecture. Three successive circumblatory galleries, the arches of which are decorated with terracotta lotus motifs. The interesting thing about the temple is that it has only a single chamber, the sanctum sanctorum, with an elongated tower, surrounded by hut shaped turrets. A passageway surrounds it and some large cannons found here date back to the Malla period. The nearest places to reach the Rasmancha are Bishnupur Museum, Mrinmoyee Mandir, Jorebangla Temple and Lal Bandh. By Air: Bishnupur is not well connected to other major cities of the country via regular flights. The nearest airport is in Kolkata at a distance of 124kms.And Netaji Subhas Chandra Airport which is at a distance of 121 km By Train: There are no regular trains to Bishnupur from other major cities of the country. The nearest Railway station is at Bankura which is located at a distance of 28kms. By Road: Panagarh is the closest bus stand to Bishnupur and is situated at a distance of 46 km.
