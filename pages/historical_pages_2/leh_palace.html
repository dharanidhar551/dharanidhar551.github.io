LEH PALACE Leh palace is a former royal palace overlooking the town of Leh, Ladakh, in the Indian Himalayas. Leh palace was constructed by Tsewang Namgyal during 17th century. It served as the regal residence of the Namgyal dynasty until the 19th century, after which the royal family permanently shifted to Stok. This 9-storey royal edifice is almost in ruins now, but it is preserved as a part of the Ladakh heritage. An example of Medieval Tibetan architecture, it has massive wars and wooden balconies. A victory tower, built in the memory of Ladakhi soldiers is present towards the top of the palace. Ancient mural paintings adorn the interiors of this palace and speak of its golden by gone area. Best places to visit near Leh Palace: 1. Shanthi Stupa: It is situated on the hill top in chanspa is beautiful dome shaped chorten called Shanti stupa. The white structure make this as an attractive tourist place in the Leh. 2. Magnetic hill: Just as the name goes, the magnetic hill is actually gravity hills which possess impeccable magnetic properties. 3. Alchi: A puny hamlet snuggled by the banks of the Indus River is the lovely village of Alchi. The roads welcome you with erect apricot trees. 4. Zanskar Valley: The snow-covered steep terrains of Zanskar Valley beckon travellers to trek on while they are overlooked with the clear blue sky. Transportation: 1. By Air: Leh Palace lies at a distance of 4.5km from Leh airport and can be easily reached by road. You can hire a taxi to reach Leh Palace. 2. By Train: Pothankot and Chandigarh are the nearest rail road stations to Leh. From there you can hire a taxi to reach Leh Palace. 3. By Road: You can go through 475km long road from Manali to Leh via Keylong, Darchu, Sarchu and Balarchala.
