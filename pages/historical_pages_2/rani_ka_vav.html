RANI KA VAV Rani Kavavor rankivav is a step-well situated in the patan in Gujarat state in India. Rani Kavav is located on the banks of Saraswati river. It is constructed as an attribution to Udayamati, daughter of Khengara of Saurashitra, queen of the 11thcentury Solanki dynasty and wife of Bhima-1. Rani Kavav was constructed during the rule of Chaulukya dynasty. According to the Prabandhachintamani, composed by the merutunga in 1304, the step well was commissioned in 1063 and was completed after 20 years. It is generally assumed that it was built in the memory of Bhima-1 by his queen Vdayamati. Best places to visit near Rani Ka Vav: 1. SahastaralingaTalav: The SahastralingaTalav is a pentagonal shape and marked by a series of mounds showind in shape. About thousands of shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva were constructed on the edge of the water tank. 2. Modhera Sun Temple: It is located at Modhera Village. This is one of the prominent heritage sites near Ahmedabad. This temple not only reflects the architectural abilities of the Solanki rules but also, speaks highly of the devotional zeal of the ruling dynasty at that time. Transportation: By Air: The nearest airport to Rani Ka Vav is Ahmadabad airport. Once traveler reached the airport he can hire auto/taxi. By Train: The nearest railhead is Mehsana. The traveler can take a bus or cab to reach Rani KaVav. By Road: Several buses and rented vehicles travel between Ahmedabad and Patna.
