FALAKNUMA PALACE INTRODUCTION:- Falaknuma is a palace in Hyderabad,Telangana. It originally belonged to the Paigah family, and was later owned by the Nizam of Hyderabad. It is on a hillock and covers a 13-hectare (32-acre) area in Falaknuma.It was built by Nawab sir viqar-ul-umra, Prime Minister of Hyderabad and the uncle & brother-in-law of the sixth Nizam. Falak-numa means "Like the Sky" or "Mirror of Sky" in Urdu. HISTORY:- The construction of the palace began in the year 1984 and the completion took 10 years. Falaknuma palace’s cost was around 4 million rupees. This beautiful structure was designed by British architect William Mard Marret and was finally owned by PM of the Hyderabad State, Sir Vicar-Ul-Umra. In 1895 Mehboob Ali Pasha, the 7th Nizam of Hyderabad took the Palace as his fancy and till 1911, Falaknuma was the residence of the Nizams of Hyderabad. Thereafter, it became a guesthouse for the Nizam’s royal guests and other dignitaries. In 2000, Prince Nizam Mukkaram Jah Bahadur leased out the palace to the Taj hotels for renovation and converted it into a luxury heritage hotel of Hyderabad. The restoration process was looked after by Princess Esra Jah, the first wife of the VII Nizam. She also built a small palace for herself close to the old building. NEAREST PLACES TO VISIT:- 1) BIRLA MANDIR:- Apart from the main shrine, the consorts of Lord Venkateswara, Padmavati and Andal are housed in separate shrines. Birla temples are open to all, as identified by Mahatma Gandhi and other Hindu leaders. It is nearly 16 kms from Falaknuma palace. 2) NEHRU ZOOLOGICAL PARK This is the most attractive place in Hyderabad. It is just 14 kms from Falanknuma palace. TRANSPORTATION FACILITIES:- BY ROAD:- From the Hyderabad number of taxies and buses are available to reach Falaknuma.
