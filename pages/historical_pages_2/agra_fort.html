About

Agra Fort Agra Fort is an historical fort located in the city of Agra in India. It is about 2.5 km northwest of its more famous sister monument, the Taj Mahal. It was the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal Dynasty till 1638, when the capital was shifted from Agra to Delhi. The Agra Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The fort can be more accurately and described as a Walled city. Agra Fort is also called as RED FORT. The fort was commissioned by Akbar in 1565 and reportedly took eight years to construct. The walls of the roughly crescent-shaped structure have a circumference of about 1.5 miles, rise 70 feet high, and are surrounded by a moat. Nearby places to reach Agra Fort are Taj Mahal and it is around 7km from Agra Fort. And Fatehpur sikri is around 38km from Agra Fort. Agra Fort railway station situated in Mantola is the nearest railway station. It is around 2 km from the Agra Fort. Alternatively you can select Idgah Bus Stand which is only 4 km from the Agra Fort. Alternative forms of transportation such as motorized rickshaws and tangas are available in the city.
