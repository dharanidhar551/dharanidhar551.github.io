Mahatma Gandhi Statue Mahatma Gandhi Statue is located in Beach Road, White Town, Pondicherry. This four-meter square statue is the biggest Mahatma Gandhi statue in Asia. The pillars were erected in 1866, while the statue, placed opposite the old Pier, was inaugurated on January 26, 1965. The four feet tall statue made of black marble. The statue captures the essence of Gandhiji, as he ventured out for his infamous Dandi March in the year 1930. The statue itself is surrounded by eight monolithic pillars. These pillars were transferred from the Gingee Fort in the year 1986, even before the statue itself was installed, which was in the year 1995. A stage flanks the Gandhi statue towards the front side. This stage is known as the Gandhi Thidal. The nearest places to reach the Mahatma Gandhi Statue are Rock Beach, Raj Niwas, Shri Aurobindo Ashram and Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Jesus.
