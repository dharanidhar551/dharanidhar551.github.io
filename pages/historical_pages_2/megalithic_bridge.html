Megalithic Bridge on the Um- Kumbeh: District: Jaintia Hills Location: Near Syndai (Lat. 25° 12′ N; Long.9209′ E) How to Reach: Airport: Shillong Railway Station: Guwahati Bus Station: Jowai It is one of the 7 most popular Historical Places in Meghalaya. Built using huge stone slabs, this megalithic bridge across Um- Kumbeh River is said to be constructed by Mar Phalangki and U Luh Lyngskor Lamare as per the local tradition. It was supposedly built by the order of Jaintia king when he shifted his capital to Nartiang in order to carry out trade between the new capital and his old capital of Sutnga. The stones used are dated to be from 18th century CE. Some other locations which are near to this Um- Kumbeh Bridge are: 1. Megalithic Bridge on the Um-Nyakanah 2. Nartiāng Monoliths 3. Kiang Nangbah 4. David Scott Memorial Stone 5. Khasi Heritage Village 6. Stone Memorial of U-Maw Thodur-Briew
